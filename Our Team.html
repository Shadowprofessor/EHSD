Professor Ajay Kumar Singh

Dr. Ajay Kumar Singh is aProfessor of Electronics and Communication Engineering at NIIT University (NU), with over 30 years of teaching and research experience. His expertise spans a wide range of fields, including submicron MOS device modeling, low-power VLSI circuit design, and renewable energy system design.

Professor Singh has contributed significantly to the development of energy harvesting systems, especially those that utilize biological processes to generate clean, renewable energy. His work continues to inspire the next generation of researchers in the field. His interest to green technology solutions can be measured by his design and development of eco-friendly, rechargeable and regenerative Herbal battery. This battery does not contain any chemical and is a favourable candidate to replace Li-ion battery in future to power low current and medium current consuming devices. At present his group is working to integrate the photosynthesis and photovoltaic techniques to create a hybrid energy harvesting system.

Dr Singh has supervised six PhDs and seven masters’ theses. He has authored two books and published more than 100 research papers in various international journals and conferences. He has successfully completed six external projects as either the Principal Investigator or co-investigator. He has published two patents on Herbal battery.

Research Areas

  • Microelectronics Circuits – Simulation and Characterization
  • Nanotechnology – Modeling of Nanoscale MOSFET, TFET, and CNTFET Devices
  • Low-Power VLSI & Memory Design
  • Renewable Energy Systems – Energy Harvesting and Power Management
  • Internet of Things (IoT)

Consulting Expertise

  • Renewable Energy – Development of sustainable energy solutions

Our Mission

To lead the advancement of sustainable energy solutions through innovative research, development, and application of energy harvesting technologies. We aim to harness renewable energy from diverse sources, including solar, wind, thermal, and bioenergy, to create efficient, eco-friendly systems that power the future.

Our mission is to foster interdisciplinary collaboration,and reduce the corbon emission by transforming how energy is generated, stored and utilized.
